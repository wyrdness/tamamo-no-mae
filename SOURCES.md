# Sources for Tamamo-no-Mae (玉藻前)

## Version Information
- **Version**: 1.0.0
- **Last Updated**: 2026-01-14

## Primary Historical Sources

### Classical Japanese Texts

1. **"Tamamo-no-Mae Monogatari"** (玉藻前物語)
   - Primary narrative source for the legend
   - Multiple manuscript versions exist
   - Medieval period composition
   - Otogi-zōshi collection inclusion

2. **"Sesshōseki no Ki"** (殺生石の記)
   - Records of the Killing Stone
   - Medieval documentation
   - Temple and shrine records
   - Local historical accounts

3. **Otogi-zōshi Collections** (御伽草子)
   - Medieval short story collections
   - Various versions of Tamamo-no-Mae tale
   - 14th-17th century compilation
   - Multiple regional variations

4. **"Konjaku Monogatarishū"** (今昔物語集)
   - 12th century tale collection
   - Fox spirit stories providing context
   - Foundation for later elaborations
   - Buddhist and secular narratives

5. **"Genpei Jōsuiki"** (源平盛衰記)
   - Historical chronicle
   - References to supernatural events at court
   - Heian period context
   - Political and supernatural intertwined

### Chinese Historical Sources

6. **"Fengshen Yanyi"** (封神演義 / "Investiture of the Gods")
   - Ming Dynasty novel (16th century)
   - Detailed Daji narrative
   - Source for Chinese incarnation
   - Widely influential text

7. **"Shiji" (Records of the Grand Historian)** (史記)
   - Sima Qian's historical work
   - Original historical account of King Zhou and Daji
   - 1st century BCE
   - Foundation for later supernatural elaborations

8. **"Shang Shu"** (尚書 / "Book of Documents")
   - Ancient Chinese historical text
   - References to Shang Dynasty fall
   - Historical basis for legends
   - Classical Chinese literature

9. **"Lienü Zhuan"** (列女傳 / "Biographies of Exemplary Women")
   - Han Dynasty text
   - Includes negative examples like Daji
   - Women and moral lessons
   - Cultural attitudes toward female power

### Buddhist and Religious Texts

10. **"Genkō Shakusho"** (元亨釈書)
    - Buddhist history of Japan
    - Records of monk Gennō
    - 14th century compilation
    - Religious perspective on legends

11. **"Gennō Zenji Gyōjōki"** (玄翁禅師行状記)
    - Biography of monk Gennō
    - Account of Sesshōseki breaking
    - Primary source for redemption story
    - Temple records

12. **Temple Records from Kyōganji** (教願寺)
    - Local temple documentation
    - Sesshōseki area history
    - Pilgrimage records
    - Religious observances

*This bibliography provides comprehensive coverage of Tamamo-no-Mae research, spanning classical texts, modern scholarship, artistic representations, and popular culture. For academic work, consulting both Japanese and Chinese language sources (items 28-37) is essential for complete understanding. The legend's cross-cultural nature requires multilingual research for full appreciation.*

---

**Research Note**: The 2022 splitting of the Sesshōseki generated substantial new documentation, including geological studies, news coverage, and social media analysis. This event provides unique opportunity to study how ancient legends remain culturally relevant and how communities respond to changes in legendary sites.
